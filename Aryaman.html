Ingredients

  • Firm or extra-firm tofu (organic if possible)
  • Olive oil or coconut oil
  • BBQ Sauce (no high-fructose corn syrup preferred)
  • Sriracha or red chili paste (optional)
  • Cabbage (green or purple)
  • Red onion
  • Fresh cilantro
  • Vegan mayo (or regular mayo)
  • Apple cider vinegar or white vinegar
  • Soft toasted buns
  • Avocado (optional)
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Cut the tofu into one-inch slabs, trimming to fit the buns. Pat dry with paper towels.
  2. Heat a skillet with oil, salt, and pepper. Sear the tofu on both sides until crispy and golden.
  3. Prepare the slaw by mixing cabbage, cilantro, onion, mayo, vinegar, salt, and pepper.
  4. Mix BBQ sauce with red chili paste (if desired). Brush the tofu slabs with the sauce, allowing it to caramelize in the pan.
  5. Toast the buns and assemble the sandwich: layer tofu, slaw, and avocado.

Tips for Success

  • For extra crunch, press the tofu before cooking to remove excess moisture.
  • Use your favorite BBQ sauce for a personalized flavor.
  • Make sure to toast the buns to add a crispy texture to your sandwich.
  • If you want a spicier version, add more sriracha or red chili paste to the BBQ sauce.

Variations

  • Grilled Tofu: Grill the tofu instead of pan-frying for a smoky flavor.
  • Slaw Variations: Add grated carrots, radishes, or apples to the slaw for extra crunch and flavor.
  • BBQ Tofu Wrap: For a lighter option, wrap the tofu and slaw in a whole wheat wrap instead of a bun.
  • Sweet Potato Fries: Serve with baked sweet potato fries for a delicious side dish.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make this recipe gluten-free?

Yes! Use gluten-free buns or serve the tofu with a gluten-free wrap or lettuce leaves instead of buns.

How do I store leftovers?

Store leftover tofu and slaw separately in airtight containers in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat tofu in a skillet to restore its crispiness.

Can I use other vegetables in the slaw?

Yes! You can add other vegetables like shredded carrots, bell peppers, or even apples for a sweet crunch.

Can I make the BBQ sauce from scratch?

Absolutely! You can make a homemade BBQ sauce by combining ketchup, apple cider vinegar, brown sugar, smoked paprika, garlic powder, and a bit of liquid smoke.
