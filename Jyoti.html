This vibrant Vegan Buddha Bowl is packed with roasted veggies, quinoa, and a creamy tahini dressing—nutritious and delicious!

Ingredients

  • Quinoa or brown rice (cooked)
  • Sweet potatoes (cubed)
  • Broccoli or cauliflower florets
  • Chickpeas (canned or cooked)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Mixed greens or baby spinach
  • Avocado (optional)
  • Sesame seeds (for garnish)
  • Fresh lemon juice
  • Tahini
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Water (to thin the dressing)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss sweet potatoes, broccoli, and chickpeas with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes until tender and golden.
  2. Prepare the quinoa or brown rice according to package instructions.
  3. Whisk together tahini, minced garlic, lemon juice, water, and a pinch of salt to make the dressing. Adjust the consistency as needed.
  4. In a large bowl, layer mixed greens, quinoa, roasted veggies, and chickpeas. Add sliced avocado if desired.
  5. Drizzle with the tahini dressing and sprinkle with sesame seeds. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Tips for the Perfect Buddha Bowl

  • Use fresh, seasonal vegetables for the best flavor.
  • Add a pinch of paprika or cumin to the roasted veggies for an extra layer of flavor.
  • Prep ingredients ahead of time to make assembly quick and easy.
  • If your tahini is too thick, warm it slightly to make it easier to whisk.

Variations

  • Protein Boost: Add grilled tofu, tempeh, or a handful of edamame.
  • Grain Swap: Use farro, bulgur, or barley instead of quinoa or rice.
  • Spicy Kick: Mix a bit of sriracha or chili paste into the tahini dressing.
  • Herb Lovers: Add fresh dill, basil, or mint for an herbal twist.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make this recipe gluten-free?

Yes! Ensure your grain (quinoa or rice) is gluten-free, and you're good to go.

How do I store leftovers?

Store components separately in airtight containers in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat the roasted veggies before assembling.

Can I use canned vegetables instead of fresh?

Fresh is best, but canned chickpeas or frozen veggies can be a quick substitute. Just ensure they are drained and patted dry before roasting.

What other dressings can I use?

You can try a peanut sauce, avocado lime dressing, or a light vinaigrette for variety.
